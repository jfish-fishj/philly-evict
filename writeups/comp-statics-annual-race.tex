\section{Overview}\label{overview}

This writeup presents annual-frequency complaint regressions using
\texttt{infousa\_pct\_black} as a proxy for building racial composition.
It addresses limitations of the quarterly analysis: InfoUSA demographics
are annual, so quarterly/monthly repetition inflates apparent precision.
All models use \textbf{Poisson fixed-effects} (\texttt{fepois}) with
\texttt{offset\ =\ log(total\_units)} to model complaint \textbf{rates}
per unit. As of the retaliatory refactor, this is the canonical location
for complaint-suppression evidence; those blocks were removed from
\texttt{writeups/retaliatory-evictions.qmd}.

\textbf{Inputs:}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \texttt{bldg\_panel\_blp} (PID \(\times\) year): demographics,
  building observables, rent, filing rates
\item
  \texttt{building\_data} (annual): complaints, permits, violations
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Key design choices:}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{Pre-COVID sample:} year range 2007--2019
\item
  \textbf{Lagged race:} all specs use \texttt{pct\_black\_l1} (one-year
  lag) to address simultaneity
\item
  Sample filter: \texttt{!is.na(infousa\_pct\_black)} only (not the
  stricter \texttt{tenant\_comp\_missing\ ==\ 0})
\item
  Maintenance controls: L1--L3 lagged repair-permit flow rates per unit
\item
  Severe violation controls: L1--L3 lagged hazardous + unsafe violation
  rates per unit
\item
  Clustering: PID level throughout
\end{itemize}

\section{Variance Decomposition}\label{variance-decomposition}

How much of the variation in \texttt{pct\_black} is between-building
versus within-building over time? If PID FE absorb nearly all variation,
then within-building specs will have little identifying power.

\begingroup\fontsize{9}{11}\selectfont

\begin{longtable}[t]{lrrrrrrrr}
\caption{\label{tab:vardecomp-table}Variance decomposition of demographic composition variables}\\
\toprule
Variable & N & PIDs & Overall SD & Between-PID SD & Within-PID SD & R2 (PID FE) & R2 (GEOID x Year) & R2 (PID + GEOID x Year)\\
\midrule
infousa\_pct\_black & 1821840 & 177421 & 0.4224 & 0.4075 & 0.1205 & 0.9098 & 0.7943 & 0.9106\\
infousa\_pct\_black\_female & 1754745 & 176717 & 0.3408 & 0.2997 & 0.1699 & 0.7236 & 0.4760 & 0.7241\\
infousa\_pct\_female & 1758534 & 176803 & 0.3634 & 0.2684 & 0.2605 & 0.4281 & 0.0169 & 0.4297\\
\bottomrule
\end{longtable}
\endgroup{}

\textbf{Interpretation:} If \texttt{R2\ (PID\ FE)} is very high (e.g.,
\(> 0.90\)), then racial composition is essentially a building-level
fixed characteristic, and within-building variation is limited. The
within-PID SD relative to overall SD indicates how much time-series
variation is available for identification under PID FE.

\section{Complaint Regression Ladder}\label{complaint-regression-ladder}

Nine specs in a single table, progressing from cross-sectional to
within-building identification. All use \texttt{pct\_black\_l1} (lagged
race). Maintenance and severe-violation controls use L1--L3 lagged flow
rates per unit.

\textbf{Cross-sectional (full sample):}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{Spec 1:}
  \texttt{pct\_black\_l1\ \textbar{}\ year\ +\ unit\_bin\_fine} --- raw
  association
\item
  \textbf{Spec 2:}
  \texttt{pct\_black\_l1\ +\ log\_total\_area\ \textbar{}\ year\ +\ tract\ +\ bldg\ obs}
  --- building + neighborhood, no maint/severity
\item
  \textbf{Spec 3:} Spec 2 +
  \texttt{maint\ L1-\/-L3\ +\ severity\ L1-\/-L3} --- adds
  maintenance/severity lags
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Rent subsample:}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{Spec 4:}
  \texttt{pct\_black\_l1\ +\ log\_total\_area\ +\ filing\_rate\ +\ log\_med\_rent\ \textbar{}\ ...}
  --- rent + filing only
\item
  \textbf{Spec 5:} Spec 3 RHS + \texttt{filing\_rate\ +\ log\_med\_rent}
  --- all controls
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Within-building (PID + year FE):}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{Spec 6:} \texttt{pct\_black\_l1\ \textbar{}\ PID\ +\ year} ---
  raw within-building
\item
  \textbf{Spec 7:}
  \texttt{pct\_black\_l1\ +\ maint\ L1-\/-L3\ +\ severity\ L1-\/-L3\ \textbar{}\ PID\ +\ year}
  --- within-building + lags
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Within-building (PID + census\_tract \(\times\) year FE):}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{Spec 8:}
  \texttt{pct\_black\_l1\ \textbar{}\ PID\ +\ tract\^{}year} ---
  within-building + market-year shocks
\item
  \textbf{Spec 9:}
  \texttt{pct\_black\_l1\ +\ maint\ L1-\/-L3\ +\ severity\ L1-\/-L3\ \textbar{}\ PID\ +\ tract\^{}year}
  --- within-building + market-year + lags
\end{itemize}

\texttt{log\_total\_area} is dropped from PID FE specs (collinear with
building FE). The key diagnostic: does the \texttt{pct\_black\_l1}
coefficient survive PID FE and tract\(\times\)year absorbers?
