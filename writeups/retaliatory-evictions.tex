\section{Overview}\label{overview}

This writeup now reports only the retaliatory timing analysis from
\texttt{r/retaliatory-evictions.r}:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  distributed-lag event-time regressions (\texttt{filed\_eviction} on
  lags/leads of complaint indicators)
\item
  bandwidth diagnostics around eviction filings
\item
  distributed-lag heterogeneity by building-level \texttt{\%\ Black}
  split
\end{itemize}

Complaint-suppression analysis was intentionally removed from this
workflow and now lives in \texttt{r/comp-statics-annual-race.R} and
\texttt{writeups/comp-statics-annual-race.qmd}. Unless explicitly
overridden in the script call, outputs shown here are pre-COVID only
(\texttt{\textless{}=\ 2019}).

\section{Empirical Bayes Filing-Rate
Memo}\label{empirical-bayes-filing-rate-memo}

The maintenance regressions below now use
\texttt{filing\_rate\_eb\_pre\_covid} as the default filing-intensity
measure. This is the building-level Empirical Bayes (Gamma-Poisson)
posterior mean pooled over pre-2019 years:

\[
y_{it} \mid \lambda_i \sim \text{Poisson}(E_{it}\lambda_i), \qquad
\lambda_i \sim \text{Gamma}(\alpha,\beta),
\]

with sufficient statistics

\[
Y_i=\sum_t y_{it}, \qquad E_i=\sum_t E_{it},
\]

and posterior mean

\[
\mathbb{E}[\lambda_i\mid Y_i,E_i]=\frac{\alpha+Y_i}{\beta+E_i}.
\]

In the production pipeline (\texttt{r/make-analytic-sample.R}, section
6b), the city prior is estimated by method-of-moments; ZIP-specific
priors are used when supported, otherwise the city prior is used as
fallback. The raw long-run filing rate remains available for robustness
and is reported in the appendix of this writeup.

Reference memo for reuse:
\texttt{docs/notes/eb-filing-rate-memo-2026-03-04.md}.

\section{Estimating Equations}\label{estimating-equations}

\subsection{Distributed lag (main)}\label{distributed-lag-main}

For complaint stem
\(c \in \{\text{complaint},\text{severe},\text{non\_severe}\}\):

\[
Y_{it}
=
\sum_{k=1}^{h}\beta^{-}_{k,c} D^c_{i,t-k}
+ \beta^{0}_{c} D^c_{i,t}
+ \sum_{k=1}^{h}\beta^{+}_{k,c} D^c_{i,t+k}
+ \alpha_i + \gamma_t + \varepsilon_{it},
\]

where:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \(Y_{it}=\mathbb{1}[\text{filed\_eviction}_{it}=1]\)
\item
  \(D^c_{it}=\mathbb{1}[\text{filed\_}c_{it}=1]\)
\item
  \(\alpha_i\): PID fixed effects, \(\gamma_t\): period fixed effects
\item
  SEs clustered at PID
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Race heterogeneity split}\label{race-heterogeneity-split}

Define:

\[
G_{it}=\mathbb{1}[\text{infousa\_pct\_black}_{it} \ge c_{\text{pre}}],
\]

where \(c_{\text{pre}}\) is computed from pre-2020 \((PID,year)\)-unique
observations using the configured rule (default: pre-COVID mean).

The same distributed-lag equation is estimated on high- and
low-\(G_{it}\) subsamples.

\section{Portfolio Eviction Group
Heterogeneity}\label{portfolio-eviction-group-heterogeneity}

Buildings are assigned to one of three portfolio eviction groups based
on a \textbf{leave-one-out (LOO)} EB pre-COVID filing rate from the
other buildings in the same conglomerate:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{Solo}: no other buildings in the conglomerate (LOO rate is
  undefined)
\item
  \textbf{Low-evicting portfolio}: LOO rate below the median across
  multi-building conglomerates
\item
  \textbf{High-evicting portfolio}: LOO rate at or above the median
\end{itemize}

This framing asks whether retaliation is more common, more severe, and
maintenance less responsive \emph{when a building's landlord operates
other high-evicting properties} --- isolating a portfolio-level
behavioral pattern rather than just the focal building's own filing
history.

\subsection{OLS Log-Rent Regression: log(Total Rent) \textbar{}
log(Ongoing
Rent)}\label{ols-log-rent-regression-logtotal-rent-logongoing-rent}

Outcome is \texttt{log(total\_rent)} with \texttt{log(ongoing\_rent)}
entered as a continuous covariate. Conditioning on log ongoing rent
removes the mechanical correlation between total back rent and rent
level across tenants. The coefficient on \texttt{retaliatory\_status}
therefore captures the percentage difference in total back rent
\emph{claimed} for retaliatory versus non-retaliatory cases, holding the
monthly rent fixed. A negative coefficient means landlords file for less
back rent in retaliatory cases --- i.e., the filing is not primarily
about recovering arrears.

\subsection{Spell-Level Maintenance
Regressions}\label{spell-level-maintenance-regressions}

Each observation is a \textbf{PID × ownership-spell} --- a contiguous
block of years during which the same conglomerate owned the parcel
(2011--2019). This collapses the \textasciitilde1.6M-row annual panel to
\textasciitilde100--250 K spells, making the cross-sectional unit honest
and covariates spell-specific (filing rate, rent, and \% Black are
averages during the landlord's tenure).

\textbf{Design notes:}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{Filing intensity} (\texttt{high\_filing\_spell}): mean of
  \texttt{filing\_rate\_eb} (pooled pre-COVID EB rate) over spell years.
  Because this EB rate is a static PID-level scalar, the spell mean
  equals the PID EB rate. Spell-varying filing intensity is available
  via the raw rate robustness column.
\item
  \textbf{FE spec}: \texttt{building\_type\ +\ start\_year\ +\ GEOID}.
  \texttt{quality\_grade}, \texttt{year\_blt\_decade}, and
  \texttt{num\_units\_bin} are included as RHS covariates rather than
  FEs. The 6-way FE spec from the annual model is too sparse at spell
  level (\textasciitilde267K spells vs \textasciitilde72M possible cells
  → all PPML singletons); this 3-way spec gives \textasciitilde14K cells
  and \textasciitilde19 spells/cell. \texttt{start\_year} absorbs permit
  trends by ownership-entry cohort.
\item
  \textbf{M4 (PID FE)}: identifies from within-parcel ownership
  transitions. \texttt{high\_filing\_spell} is collinear with PID FE (EB
  rate does not vary by year) and is dropped automatically by
  \texttt{fixest}. Identification comes from
  \texttt{portfolio\_evict\_group\_cong} and
  \texttt{above\_mean\_pct\_black\_spell}, which vary across spells if
  conglomerate composition or pct\_Black changes at ownership
  transition.
\item
  \textbf{LOO classification} (\texttt{portfolio\_evict\_group\_cong}):
  computed from the \textbf{full 2011--2019 panel} (not the spell
  window) via \texttt{compute\_loo\_filing\_type()}. Landlord type is a
  structural attribute; full-period averaging reduces noise. Reference =
  ``Solo''. Threshold: 0.05 filings/unit-year; small-portfolio screen:
  \texttt{loo\_min\_units\ ×\ 9} unit-years.
\end{itemize}
